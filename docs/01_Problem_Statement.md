Echo
Problem Statement
Background

In today's digital world, ideas, inspirations, notes, and reflections are scattered across multiple applications such as Notes, WhatsApp, Google Keep, Pinterest, browser bookmarks, and notebooks. This fragmentation makes it difficult for users to retrieve information, identify relationships between ideas, and build upon previous thoughts.
Instead of helping people think better, existing tools often become isolated storage spaces.

Problem
People capture information in many different places, causing:
Important ideas to be forgotten.
Difficulty finding previously saved information.
No meaningful connection between related thoughts.
Information overload from multiple platforms.
Reduced creativity due to disconnected knowledge.
Proposed Solution

Echo is a digital second brain that enables users to capture ideas, organize knowledge, and visually connect related thoughts within a single workspace.

Rather than acting as a simple note-taking application, Echo helps users discover relationships between ideas, making knowledge easier to revisit, expand, and apply.

Target Users:
Students
Lifelong learners
Creatives
Designers
Developers
Writers
Researchers
Anyone who frequently captures ideas
Expected Outcome

Echo aims to reduce information fragmentation by providing a unified platform where ideas evolve into interconnected knowledge.
